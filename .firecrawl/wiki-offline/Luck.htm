Luck

Luck is one of the stats in Vampire Survivors. It affects various random chance events, giving the player higher odds at positive outcomes.

The player has 100% base Luck before modifications, such as the character's bonuses or PowerUps. The Luck in the stat panel is given as the difference from base Luck, meaning that a Luck stat of +30% equates to 130% total Luck. Luck modifications stack additively.

There is no upper limit to how much Luck the player may have.

Effects

[edit | edit source]

Luck increases the following:

  • The chance of getting a fourth option to choose from when leveling up
  • The chance of getting pickups other than  Gold Coins or  Coin Bags when destroying light sources
  • The respawn rate of light sources
  • The chance of getting more than one reward from a  Treasure Chest
  • The chance of positive weapon effects triggering, which includes:
    • Dealing critical hits
    • Freezing, slowing down or defanging enemies
    •  Cherry Bomb exploding
    •  Gatti Amari attacking, starting a scuffle and activating certain pickups on the player instead of wasting them or causing a detrimental effect on themselves
    •  Vicious Hunger turning enemies and pickups into Gold Coins
    • The chance of firing more Amount of  Phas3r and  Photonstorm
  • The damage of  108 Bocce  if Luck is higher than  Curse
  • The spawn chance of light sources and the critical hit damage of  Greatest Jubilee's light show finisher
  •  Sapphire Mist (I) chance to fire weapons multiple times
  • The chance of spawning additional stage items
  • The chance to be offered weapons already in your inventory
  • The cooldown of traps in Dairy Plant and Gallo Tower

Luck reduces the following:

  • The chance of map events, such as Swarms and Flower Walls, taking place
  • The chance of negative weapon effects triggering, which includes:
    •  Gatti Amari to attack the player
    •  Pentagram destroying  Experience Gems, Treasure Chests and Pickups
  • The amount of attacks by  Lucky Swipe 
  • The odds of obtaining  Gold Finger

From items

[edit | edit source]

Luck's associated passive item is the  Clover, which provides +10% Luck per level, up to a maximum of +50% Luck at level 5.

Additionally,  Little Clover pickup increases Luck by 10% when collected.

 Golden Egg permanently increases Luck by 1% for the character that collected it.

From arcana

[edit | edit source]

 Wicked Season (XIII) doubles Luck in certain 10 second intervals, and gives +0.5% Luck every level.

  •  Toastie start with +100% Luck, and gains +20% Luck every 10 levels, up to +100% at level 50.
  •  Smith start with +100% Luck, and gains +0.7% Luck every level, with no cap.

Emergency Meeting

  •  Henry gains up to +100% Luck depending on the number of enemies in sight.

From stages

[edit | edit source]

The hyper modes of all stages, except Mad Forest, Holy Forbidden, The Bone Zone, and Eudaimonia Machine, introduce a Luck bonus:

  • +10% from Inlaid Library, Dairy Plant, Gallo Tower, Cappella Magna, Green Acres, Boss Rash, Bat Country, and Tiny Bridge.
  • +20% from Il Molise and Moongolow.

Legacy of the Moonspell

[edit | edit source]
  • +10% from Mt.Moonspell.

The inverse modes of all stages, except Holy Forbidden and Eudaimonia Machine give a +20% luck bonus additively.

Mechanics

[edit | edit source]

TotalLuck

[edit | edit source]

TotalLuck is Internal variable used for the following formulas, and is equal to the displayed luck percentage plus one hundred.

  • For example, most characters start with +30% Luck with the Luck Powerup maxed out, which corresponds to a TotalLuck variable of 130.

Fourth level up option

[edit | edit source]

It is possible to sometimes gain four item choices upon leveling up. This depends on the character's Luck. The chance to receive a fourth option is:

chanceFourth=1−(1totalLuck)

Higher rarity pickups

[edit | edit source]

Luck increases the chance to get pickups other than  Gold Coins and  Coin Bags from light sources by increasing their weight (="rarity") in the item pool:

itemWeight=rarity⋅totalLuck

Respawn rate of light sources

[edit | edit source]

Each stage introduces a chance of spawning light sources just out of sight every second, which is increased by Luck:

destructibleSpawnChance=mapDestructibleChance⋅totalLuck

Treasure Chest level

[edit | edit source]

Each Treasure Chest has a base level, ranging from 1-3 uniquely for each chest and carries 3 separate chances for a level 3, 2, and 1 treasure, these chances are also unique to each chest. The game checks for each level of treasure, starting from level 3 (5 item chest) proceeding to a lower level if the previous check fails. If all attempts fail, the chests base level is used. Luck increases the chance of each check succeeding:

chanceWithLuck=levelChance⋅totalLuck

Chance based weapon effects

[edit | edit source]

Weapon effects, such as critical hits from Bloody Tear or Pentagram's item erasing, that are based on a chance are affected by Luck. The probability of these effects triggering is:

chanceWithLuck=positiveEffectChance⋅totalLuck

Additional stage items

[edit | edit source]

Some stage items can spawn in multiples in Mad Forest and Inlaid Library. The chance of additional items spawning is increased by Luck and can be calculated as:

spawnChance=1−chancetotalLuck

Note, that this check is performed by only once at the start of the run and is thus only affected by your starting Luck from character, PowerUp and stage bonuses.

Owned items on level up

[edit | edit source]

The chance to be offered items already in your inventory is affected by Luck as follows:

ownedChance=1+0.3x−1totalLuck

where x=2 if the player's level is even and x=1 if the level is odd. The check is performed twice and, if successful, an item already owned by the player will be randomly selected to appear as an option for each successful check. However, if the second roll selects the same item as the first it discarded and a new item is pulled from the pool of all items.

Skipping events

[edit | edit source]

Most map events feature a chance for it to happen, which is unique to each event. Luck affects the chance as follows:

chanceWithLuck=eventChancetotaLluck

Trap cooldown

[edit | edit source]

Luck increases the cooldown of traps as follows:

effectiveCooldown=trapGlobalCooldown⋅totalLuck

Tips

[edit | edit source]
  • Luck bonuses stack additively. The highest possible Luck stat without picking up any Little Clovers is (player level + 200)%. It can be obtained by playing as Alamaric Sniper, who starts with +100% and gains +1% per level, with 3 levels of the Luck PowerUp (+30%), 5 levels of Clover (+50%), and playing on Il Molise or Moongolow on hyper mode (+20%).
  • In terms of dealing damage and surviving, Luck benefits seem weak, but it has a large influence on many other game mechanics. These effects are extremely desirable and can be a large difference maker in a run.
  • Due to how Luck's effect is calculated, some chance based events can become guaranteed if the player has enough Luck. For example, +54% Luck would be enough to completely eliminate the chance of Pentagram erasing items past level 7.
