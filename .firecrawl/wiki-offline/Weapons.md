**Weapons** are used by the [character](Character.md) to fend off [enemies](Enemy.md), each having its own unique way of doing so. Most weapons deal damage to enemies, while some apply debuffs to enemies or buffs to the player.

The player can select up to six different weapons from level-ups, but it is possible to carry more by picking up weapons that [generate with the stage](Passive_item.md#Stage_items). Most weapons must be unlocked before they can be offered to the player.

Most weapons are unique, which means a character can only gain a weapon once per run, and there is no way to get the same weapon multiple times (even if it is consumed). Exceptions include single-use weapons like  [Candybox](Candybox.md) or  [Arma Dio](Arma_Dio.md).

## Obtaining {#Obtaining}

Normally, each character has access to one of the base weapons as their starting weapon and is able to obtain additional base weapons as they [level up](Level_up.md). Rarity determines the [weight](Level_up.md#Mechanics) of how often that weapon shows up in the pool of possible weapons and [passive items](Passive_item.md) during the level-up screen and when opening  [Treasure Chests](Treasure_Chest.md).

### Upgrading {#Upgrading}

There is a certain [chance](Level_up.md#Receiving_owned_items) for weapons that the player has already owned to be offered to them upon level-up. If an already owned weapon is selected, it will be upgraded to a higher level, and it will gain certain stat bonuses unique to the weapon. When a weapon is fully leveled, it cannot appear in the level-up options anymore. Upgrading a weapon will also reset its  [Cooldown](Cooldown.md) and activate it instantly, which is useful for powerful weapons with long Cooldown.

### Evolution {#Evolution}

Main article: [Evolution](Evolution.md)

When a base weapon is fully leveled and its corresponding passive item is obtained, it can be transformed into an evolved weapon by collecting a  [Treasure Chest](Treasure_Chest.md). Some weapons only need to be fully leveled for them to evolve. Not all weapons have an evolution.

Evolution actually works by removing the base weapon and acquiring the evolved one (with a few exceptions), which means whenever a weapon is evolved, all the effects of the original weapon will be lost, and the evolved weapon\'s Cooldown starts from zero (which is noticeable for weapons with long Cooldown, like [Santa Water](Santa_Water.md)). Evolved weapons also remove the base weapon regardless of whether obtained by normal evolution rules or not (for example, obtaining an evolution by  [Super Candybox II Turbo](Super_Candybox_II_Turbo.md) will also remove its base weapon).

Certain characters ( [Mortaccio](Mortaccio.md),  [Yatta Cavallo](Yatta_Cavallo.md),  [Bianca Ramba](Bianca_Ramba.md), and  [O\'Sole Meeo](O'Sole_Meeo.md)) automatically evolve their starting weapon when they morph into a new form, provided that the player has obtained the required [relic](Relic.md) and met the necessary condition.

## Stats {#Stats}

Weapons have different stats, which define their functions.

See [Overview Stats](Weapons/Overview_Stats.md) for a list of base stats of the weapons.

| **Base Damage** | The damage dealt by a single projectile per hit. Modified by [Might](Might.md) stat.^[\[1\]](#cite_note-1)^ | **Area** | The base area of the weapon. Modified by [Area](Area.md) stat. |
| **Speed** | The base projectile speed of the weapon. Modified by [Speed](Speed.md) stat. | **Amount** | The amount of projectiles fired per use. Modified by [Amount](Amount.md) stat. |

### Evolutions {#Evolutions}

Traditionally, evolved weapons cannot be upgraded, only have one level, and have a rarity of 1. However, newer expansion content such as the [Emerald Diorama](Emerald_Diorama.md) breaks many of the older conventions for weapon evolutions. Notable exceptions of older weapons that broke these conventions are the Bi-Bracelet, Tri-Bracelet, Alucard Swords, Vol Confodere, Melio Confodere, Vol Luminatio, and Vol Umbra. Many newer weapons also have additional secondary evolutions.
