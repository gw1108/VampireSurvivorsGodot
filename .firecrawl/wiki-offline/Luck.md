# Luck {#firstHeading .firstHeading .mw-first-heading}

[Jump to navigation](#mw-head) [Jump to search](#searchInput)

<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File">

</figure>

**Luck** is one of the [stats](Player_stats.md) in *[Vampire Survivors](Vampire_Survivors.md)*. It affects various random chance events, giving the player higher odds at positive outcomes.

The player has 100% base Luck before modifications, such as the character\'s bonuses or [PowerUps](PowerUps.md). The Luck in the stat panel is given as the difference from base Luck, meaning that a Luck stat of +30% equates to 130% total Luck. Luck modifications stack additively.

There is no upper limit to how much Luck the player may have.

## Effects {#Effects}

Luck increases the following:

- The chance of getting a fourth option to choose from when leveling up
- The chance of getting [pickups](Pickups.md) other than  [Gold Coins](Gold_Coin.md) or  [Coin Bags](Coin_Bag.md) when destroying [light sources](Light_source.md)
- The respawn rate of [light sources](Light_source.md)
- The chance of getting more than one reward from a  [Treasure Chest](Treasure_Chest.md)
- The chance of positive [weapon](Weapon.md) effects triggering, which includes:
  - Dealing critical hits
  - [Freezing](Freeze.md), slowing down or [defanging](Defang.md) enemies
  -  [Cherry Bomb](Cherry_Bomb.md) exploding
  -  [Gatti Amari](Gatti_Amari.md) attacking, starting a scuffle and activating certain [pickups](Pickups.md) on the player instead of wasting them or causing a detrimental effect on themselves
  -  [Vicious Hunger](Vicious_Hunger.md) turning [enemies](Enemies.md) and pickups into Gold Coins
  - The chance of firing more Amount of  [Phas3r](Phas3r.md) and  [Photonstorm](Photonstorm.md)

<!-- -->

- The damage of  [108 Bocce](108_Bocce.md)  if Luck is higher than  [Curse](Curse.md)
- The spawn chance of light sources and the critical hit damage of  [Greatest Jubilee](Greatest_Jubilee.md)\'s light show finisher
-  [Sapphire Mist (I)](Sapphire_Mist_(I).md) chance to fire weapons multiple times
- The chance of spawning additional [stage items](Stage_item.md)
- The chance to be offered weapons already in your inventory
- The cooldown of [traps](Map_event.md) in [Dairy Plant](Dairy_Plant.md) and [Gallo Tower](Gallo_Tower.md)

Luck reduces the following:

- The chance of [map events](Map_event.md), such as [Swarms](Swarm.md) and [Flower Walls](Flower_Wall.md), taking place
- The chance of negative weapon effects triggering, which includes:
  -  [Gatti Amari](Gatti_Amari.md) to attack the player
  -  [Pentagram](Pentagram.md) destroying  [Experience Gems](Experience_Gem.md), [Treasure Chests](Treasure_Chest.md) and [Pickups](Pickup.md)
- The amount of attacks by  [Lucky Swipe](Lucky_Swipe.md) 
- The odds of obtaining  [Gold Finger](Gold_Finger.md)

## From items {#From_items}

Luck\'s associated [passive item](Passive_item.md) is the  [Clover](Clover.md), which provides +10% Luck per level, up to a maximum of +50% Luck at level 5.

Additionally,  [Little Clover](Little_Clover.md) pickup increases Luck by 10% when collected.

 [Golden Egg](Golden_Egg.md) permanently increases Luck by 1% for the character that collected it.

## Mechanics {#Mechanics}

### TotalLuck {#TotalLuck}

TotalLuck is Internal variable used for the following formulas, and is equal to the displayed luck percentage plus one hundred.

- For example, most characters start with +30% Luck with the Luck Powerup maxed out, which corresponds to a TotalLuck variable of 130.

### Fourth level up option {#Fourth_level_up_option}

It is possible to sometimes gain four item choices upon [leveling up](Level_up.md). This depends on the character\'s Luck. The chance to receive a fourth option is:

$chanceFourth = 1 - \left( \frac{1}{totalLuck} \right)$

### Higher rarity pickups {#Higher_rarity_pickups}

Luck increases the chance to get [pickups](Pickups.md) other than  [Gold Coins](Gold_Coin.md) and  [Coin Bags](Coin_Bag.md) from [light sources](Light_source.md) by increasing their weight (=\"rarity\") in the item pool:

$itemWeight = rarity \cdot totalLuck$

### Respawn rate of light sources {#Respawn_rate_of_light_sources}

Each [stage](Stage.md) introduces a chance of spawning [light sources](Light_source.md) just out of sight every second, which is increased by Luck:

$destructibleSpawnChance = mapDestructibleChance \cdot totalLuck$

### Treasure Chest level {#Treasure_Chest_level}

Each [Treasure Chest](Treasure_Chest.md) has a base level, ranging from 1-3 uniquely for each chest and carries 3 separate chances for a level 3, 2, and 1 treasure, these chances are also unique to each chest. The game checks for each level of treasure, starting from level 3 (5 item chest) proceeding to a lower level if the previous check fails. If all attempts fail, the chests base level is used. Luck increases the chance of each check succeeding:

$chanceWithLuck = levelChance \cdot totalLuck$

### Chance based weapon effects {#Chance_based_weapon_effects}

[Weapon](Weapon.md) effects, such as critical hits from [Bloody Tear](Bloody_Tear.md) or [Pentagram](Pentagram.md)\'s item erasing, that are based on a chance are affected by Luck. The probability of these effects triggering is:

$chanceWithLuck = positiveEffectChance \cdot totalLuck$

### Additional stage items {#Additional_stage_items}

Some [stage items](Stage_item.md) can spawn in multiples in [Mad Forest](Mad_Forest.md) and [Inlaid Library](Inlaid_Library.md). The chance of additional items spawning is increased by Luck and can be calculated as:

$spawnChance = 1 - \frac{chance}{totalLuck}$

Note, that this check is performed by only once at the start of the run and is thus only affected by your starting Luck from [character](Character.md), [PowerUp](PowerUp.md) and [stage](Stage.md) bonuses.

### Owned items on level up {#Owned_items_on_level_up}

The chance to be offered items already in your inventory is affected by Luck as follows:

$ownedChance = 1 + 0.3x - \frac{1}{totalLuck}$

where *x=2* if the player\'s level is even and *x=1* if the level is odd. The check is performed twice and, if successful, an item already owned by the player will be randomly selected to appear as an option for each successful check. However, if the second roll selects the same item as the first it discarded and a new item is pulled from the pool of all items.

### Skipping events {#Skipping_events}

Most [map events](Map_events.md) feature a chance for it to happen, which is unique to each event. Luck affects the chance as follows:

$chanceWithLuck = \frac{eventChance}{totaLluck}$

### Trap cooldown {#Trap_cooldown}

Luck increases the cooldown of traps as follows:

$effectiveCooldown = trapGlobalCooldown \cdot totalLuck$
