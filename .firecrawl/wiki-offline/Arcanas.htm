"Cards" redirects here. For Vampire Crawlers content, see Crawlers:Cards.

Arcana cards are a modifier system in Vampire Survivors. Obtaining the  Randomazzo in Gallo Tower unlocks the first Arcana,  Sarabande of Healing (VI).

The rest of the Arcanas in the base set are unlocked by reaching level 50 with certain characters, or by reaching the 31:00 mark in certain stages. Obtaining the  Darkasso in Room 1665 unlocks an additional set of "Darkanas", allowing even more Arcanas to be unlocked by achieving specific tasks with certain other characters.

Mechanics

[edit | edit source]

Each Arcana modifies gameplay in a particular way. The player can choose to enable or disable Arcanas in the stage selection screen.

With Arcanas enabled, the player can obtain up to three Arcanas over the course of a run (although through certain means, more than three can be obtained):

  1. At the beginning of a run, the player is allowed to choose one of any unlocked Arcanas or Darkanas. They are also given the option to select a random one.
  2. At 11:00 and 21:00, a special boss will spawn that, upon death, who drops an  Arcana Chest. Upon being picked up, the chest will allow the player to choose an additional Arcana. The menu offers four or more randomly chosen Arcanas from the uncollected Arcanas. The player may spend Rerolls to get new choices until all options have been displayed, and unlike normal level ups, previous options will not be offered again.

If the player has three or more Arcanas, the Arcana bosses will instead drop regular chests. If Arcanas are disabled, the Arcana bosses will not appear in the stage.

If the player has unlocked a total of 23 or more Arcanas or Darkanas (which requires unlocking at least one Darkana), Arcana Chests will instead offer six random Arcanas as opposed to four, and each Arcana Chest will allow the player one free Reroll.

In stages where the time limit is not 30:00, the Arcana bosses may appear at different times. In Boss Rash, Bat Country, and Tiny Bridge, the bosses instead spawn at 5:00 and 10:00.

Some characters automatically start with Arcana effects, such as  Avatar Infernas starting with  Heart of Fire (XIX); these Arcana effects do not take up an Arcana slot, and they will still be present if Arcanas are disabled.

Obtaining more than three Arcanas

[edit | edit source]

There is no limit to the amount of Arcanas a player can have at one time, although more unique methods are required for obtaining more than three.

  •  Queen Sigma is allowed to select an additional Arcana at levels 1, 2, 3, 77, and 108. All unlocked Arcanas are available as choices.
  • If Inverse mode is active, the  Merchant will sell an Arcana for 20,000, regardless of how many Arcanas the player currently has. All unlocked Arcanas are available as choices.
    • With Endless mode enabled, the Merchant will respawn with another for sale at the start of each normal game length cycle (30 minutes in most stages). This can be repeated until all unlocked Arcanas are collected.
  • Treasure Chests dropped by bosses spawned by  Moonlight Bolero (VI) can occasionally contain Arcanas. This chance remains until all unlocked Arcanas have been collected.

Darkanas

[edit | edit source]

Darkanas were introduced in patch 1.11 and are unlocked by obtaining the  Darkasso found due south in Room 1665. There are currently 12 Darkanas total. Each Darkana is also represented by a Roman numeral.

While Darkanas often share similar themes to their "counterpart" Arcanas, whether or not they're meant to be the inverse or opposite of Arcana is merely speculation, and has never been confirmed. However, this is likely false, as the patch notes for the same version that introduced Darkana state "The only difference between Arcanas and Darkanas is their theme, functionally they are exactly the same," [1] with no other confirmation toward this intention being given.

Trivia

[edit | edit source]
  • Arcanas can be unlocked by typing the code "randomazzami" into the Secrets menu, granting the player the Randomazzo and Sarabande of Healing (VI).
  • Darkanas can be unlocked by typing the code "darkassami" into the Secrets menu, granting the player the Darkasso and Moonlight Bolero (VI).
  • All of the "Reach minute 31 in the [stage]" Arcanas (Mad Groove (VIII), Out of Bounds (XII), Disco of Gold (XV), and Silent Old Sanctuary (XX)), as well as Game Killer (0), can be unlocked by typing "aintnobodygottimeforthat" while on the main menu.
  • This deck of Arcanas is loosely based on the real Major Arcana used in Tarot card readings. For example, Boogaloo of Illusions (XVIII)'s real counterpart is The Moon (XVIII), which represents intuition, loneliness, and illusion.
  • In normal stages, the Arcanas are dropped by a Glowing Bat boss.
  • Placeholders for all Arcanas existed in the game files before all Arcana were officially released, with the unreleased ones having placeholder names being musical tracks from the Castlevania series. Most of them were slightly modified before being officially released. For example, what was once place held as "Tragic Prince" has since been released as Tragic Princess (III).
  • The pictures on the Darkanas are based on the pictures on their Randomazzo counterparts. For example, Gemini (I) and Sapphire Mist (I) depict the same two figures.
  • The correct singular form of the word is "arcanum", whereas "arcana" is already in plural. However, in-game, "arcana" is used to refer to a single card and "arcanas" is used for multiple.
  • With Game Killer (0), all treasure chests will always contain at least three rewards, allowing Arcana Chests to contain two additional chest rewards.
