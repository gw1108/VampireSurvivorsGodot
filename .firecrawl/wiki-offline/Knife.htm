Knife is a weapon in Vampire Survivors. It is the starting weapon of  Gennaro Belpaese, and one of the optional starting weapons of  Zephyr . It is unlocked by default.

Its evolution is  Thousand Edge, which requires  Bracer.

As a vampire-killing tool, it can be picked from  Coat of Arms .

Effects

Knife throws projectiles in the direction the player is moving or last moved. It ignores  Duration and has limited benefit from  Area.

Multiple knives from  Amount are fired in short volleys in parallel. The firing Interval is decreased on levels 4, 6 and 8, grouping each volley of knives closer together. Each knife can initially hit one enemy before dissipating. They cannot pass through walls.

While playing with a keyboard, the knives can only be shot out in eight cardinal and ordinal directions. When playing with a mouse or gamepad, the knives can be fired in any direction.

Arcanas

  •  Iron Blue Will (VII) - Allows Knife's projectiles to bounce off enemies up to three times, occasionally have infinite Pierce, and pass through walls.
  •  Slash (XVI) - Allows Knife to critical hit. Its critical hits deal 6x damage and have 30% chance to occur. This chance is affected by  Luck.
  •  Pale Diamond Incursion (V) - Aims at the nearest enemy instead of the faced direction, and gains  Cooldown bonus based on  Speed.

Tips

  • Knife can deal critical damage after  Slash (XVI) is picked. The more  Luck you have, the more likely you are to deal critical hits. Luck can be increased with  Clover,  Little Clovers, or the Luck PowerUp.
  • No matter how you control, Knife is one of the best options for single target damage and keeping enemies at bay in the whole game because of its ability to direct the knives, its unlimited range, and its high  Amount and low  Cooldown.

Trivia

  • Knife is the first weapon to have level ups that decrease Interval.
  • This weapon is inspired by the Dagger sub-weapon from the Castlevania series. Leveling up Knife focuses on increasing  Amount, likely a reference to the upgrades from those games which usually increase the amount of daggers thrown as well.
