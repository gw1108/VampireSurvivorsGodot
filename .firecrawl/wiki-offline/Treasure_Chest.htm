Treasure Chests are items that can be dropped by bosses. They can contain a number of things, including weapons, passive items, coins, and, if the conditions are met, evolutions.

Mechanics

[edit | edit source]

Prize amount

[edit | edit source]

Chests will most commonly contain one item, but have a chance to contain three or five items instead, depending on Luck. Typically, chests will contain a random weapon or passive item the player currently has, leveling up the item by one. Chests containing more than one item may contain the same item multiple times, in which case the item will be leveled up the appropriate amount of times. In addition to whatever items a chest contains, the player will also receive a random amount of gold:

  • 1-item chests reward between 100 - 200 gold at base.
  • 3-item chests reward between 300 - 600 gold at base.
  • 5-item chests reward between 500 - 1,000 gold at base.

The chances for obtaining a 1, 3, or 5-item chest are individually configured per boss enemy. Using the first Silver Bat that appears in Mad Forest at minute 9:00 as an example, it has a treasure config that looks like the following:

Level: 1
Evolution: No
3% 10% 50%
Rewards:
  1. Upgrade (weapon)
  2. Upgrade (any)
  3. Upgrade (any)
  4. Upgrade (weapon)
  5. Upgrade (weapon)

This means, before factoring in the Luck value, there is a base 3% chance for a 5-item chest, and if that roll fails then a 10% chance for a 3-item chest, and if that roll fails then a 50% chance for a 1-item chest. If the third roll also fails, then this specific Silver Bat has a level 1 fallback, so it will drop a level 1 (1-item) chest if all 3 rolls fail. The chance for each roll including Luck is as follow:

chanceWithLuck=levelChance⋅totalLuck

Where levelChance is each of the percentages defined in the enemy's treasure config. The chanceWithLuck is compared against a randomly generated number between 0 and 100, with each check having a separate random number.

Prize types

[edit | edit source]

When the requirements for an evolution (or union) are fulfilled, certain chests will be able to evolve (or unite) a weapon. These chests typically drop from bosses that spawn after 10:00, or the respective time in a cycle of endless mode, but certain maps or bosses will be able to evolve before this point. If the player has the Yellow Sign, any chest that is able to evolve will be silver. Generally, only one weapon can be evolved per chest, including the higher tier chests. Much like the ability to evolve, there are also a multitude of exceptions for chests that are able to evolve multiple weapons.

  • For examples of which chests, from before 10:00, that can evolve weapon, see the evolution page.

If all items are at max level and no evolutions are possible, all items in regular chests are replaced with Big Coin Bags ( 25). The Treasure Chest will also randomize the amount the player will receive, which is normally around 150, but this number grows based on how many items the player received in said chest and multiplied by Greed.

Additionally, if the player has not yet received a  Candybox from Treasure Chests or level ups, and all items have been replaced with Big Coin Bags, then each Coin Bag in a Treasure Chest will have a 1% chance to award the player with a Candybox. This chance can be increased by  Luck, and can only happen once per run. The player is guaranteed this reward from Treasure Chests that award five items.

  • Once the player has obtained a Candybox via any means, then this chance will instead award a  Super Candybox II Turbo. Note that Super Candybox II Turbos can only be awared in evolution chests, as it is a gift.

Beginner's luck

[edit | edit source]

The first six chests picked up in a save will always drop items in the set sequence 1-1-3-1-1-5. These first six chests' animations cannot be skipped. After the first six chests, the animations of chests with one or three items can be skipped. Five-item chests' animations will be skippable once the player has seen the final fireworks, collected 50 five-item chests, or collected a total of 500 chests.

Skipping

[edit | edit source]

As mentioned prior, one-item and three-item chests will be skippable once the player has opened seven chests, with five-item chests being skippable later. Once the player unlocks the ability to skip five-item chests, any chests that contain only Big Coin Bags will have their animation automatically skipped in the overworld, granting the player the coins immediately.

Once the player has unlocked  Edge of the Earth (VIII), by triggering a Lavatrix Machina as  Para Kooleo after obtaining the  Darkasso, they will unlock the option to toggle "Quick Treasures" in options during a run. This will cause all chests to avoid opening the menu, instantly granting the prizes they normally would have, much like they would if they only contained gold. "Quick Treasures" does not affect the aforementioned mechanic for skipping gold-only chests.

Types

[edit | edit source]

Bronze

[edit | edit source]

Bronze chests are usually incapable of providing weapon evolutions or unions. They are typically obtained as drops from bosses spawned before 10:00.

Silver

[edit | edit source]

Silver chests are capable of providing weapon evolutions and unions. They are typically obtained as drops from bosses spawned after 10:00.

Gold

[edit | edit source]

Golden chests are capable of providing weapons the player does not currently have, including base versions of equipped evolutions/unions, and will have those weapons appear as stage items that must be physically picked up.  Zi'Appunta Belpaese can have these spawn from her special light sources with a  Luck-based chance. Chests may also be upgraded to this type via  Call of a Mad Moon (XIII).

Arcana

[edit | edit source]

Arcana chests provide the player with an Arcana. They will offer to choose one out of 4 or 6 Arcana options depending on the amount of Arcanas unlocked. Will provide two additional items if the player has  Game Killer (0). They are typically obtained as drops from bosses that spawn at 11:00 and 21:00. If two Arcanas are obtained from these chests while extra Arcana chests exist on the stage, they will turn into bronze chests. If a newer player has all of their unlocked arcanas in use (say that they have only unlocked 1-2 arcanas), any extra Arcana chests will act like Silver chests instead.

Dark

[edit | edit source]

Dark chests have a 50% chance to function identically as bronze chests, except they will always contain only one item. The other 50% of the time, dark chests provide the player with a new passive item that they do not currently have which is not banished or sealed, whether all of the player's items are max level or not. However, if all of the available passive items are already obtained, the new passive option cannot upgrade any existing weapons or passive items so they only provide coins. If the player has Game Killer (0), dark chests can provide multiple new passive items at once. They can only be obtained as drops from  Levarsee Darkasso spawned by  Moonlight Bolero (VI) or  The Blinder spawned by  Call of a Mad Moon (XIII).

Audio

[edit | edit source]

Jingles

[edit | edit source]

When the player finds a chest, Treasure Found jingle will play. Then one of the following jingles would play, depending if the treasure chest contains 1, 3, or 5 items:

  • Treasure A jingle (for a 1-item treasure chest)
  • Treasure B jingle (for a 3-item treasure chest)
  • Treasure C jingle (for a 5-item treasure chest)

If the player went to Options and enabled "Blast Processed" sound effects, Blast Processed Treasure Found jingle will play instead. Then one these jingles would play:

  • Blast Processed Treasure A jingle (for a 1-item treasure chest)
  • Blast Processed Treasure B jingle (for a 3-item treasure chest)
  • Blast Processed Treasure C jingle (for a 5-item treasure chest)

Trivia

[edit | edit source]
  • A treasure chest appears during the game's loading screen.
  • When a player completes a round having collected at least one treasure chest that contains 5 items, the pickups section of the results screen will display a golden chest. The silver chest in that section would indicate chests containing 3 items.[Verify]
  • The jingles that play when obtaining a 1-item chest, 3-item chest, or a 5-item chest, along with their variations, are all composed and arranged by William Davies, the same person who made "The Beginning" soundtrack.
  • Purple/arcana chests are indicated by a purple arrow while all of the other different kinds of chests are indicated by a yellow/golden arrow.
    • Likewise, in the  Milky Way Map, purple/arcana chests are uniquely indicated as purple while every other chest is indicated as a bronze chest.
  • The golden treasure chest is the only chest where its items are not automatically given to the player. Instead, its items appear as pickups near the location of the chest.
