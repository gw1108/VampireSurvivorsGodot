Dungeons

Stages are the playing fields where the player fights off hordes of enemies to survive as long as possible until the stage's time limit.

The game currently features 80 official stages, divided into 33 primary stages and 47 adventure mode-exclusive stages. The primary stages include 20 stages from the base game, consisting of 5 normal stages, 7 bonus stages, 9 challenge stages, 2 special stages, and 1 hidden stage, as well as 9 additional stages from the DLCs. Adventure mode, on the other hand, includes 13 stages from the base game and 34 stages from all DLC adventures combined (not counting the identical repeating stages of the main game). Stages are unlocked by completing specific requirements tied to their corresponding achievements, without the need to be bought with coins. The exception is Mad Forest, which is the first playable stage and is unlocked by default.

Overview

[edit | edit source]

Stages are the primary areas where players fight against enemy waves, with the goal of surviving until the time limit ends, which is at 30:00, 20:00, or 15:00 depending on the stage.

Stages differ in their layout and structure. Some, such as Mad Forest, Green Acres, and The Bone Zone, feature open areas that allow players to move freely. Others, like Inlaid Library, Gallo Tower, and Tiny Bridge, have more confined designs, restricting movement to specific directions. Stages also vary in their environmental features, with some containing obstacles, walls, or corridors that require players to navigate carefully. Certain stages include interactive elements, such as trap pizzas, which are pressure plates that trigger map events when activated, or the teleporting doors in Astral Stair, which lead to rooms accessible only through a specific door.

Players can interact with various objects found in stages. Destructibles are common and can be broken by hitting them with the player’s weapons. The most frequently encountered destructible is the light source, which appears in all stages and drops a random pickup item when destroyed. Some stages also contain unique destructibles, such as the Dairy Carts in Dairy Plant or the levers in Laborratory. Additionally, stage items are pre-placed objects that players can collect, including weapons, passive items, pickups, and relics. Other notable features are the coffins, which are found in certain stages and can be opened to unlock new characters, and the Merchant, which allows the player to purchase rare weapons, power-ups, Golden Eggs, and more.

Completing a stage

[edit | edit source]

If the player survives until the stage's time limit, the stage is considered complete, at which point one Reaper will arrive every minute to slay the player and end their run, unless Endless mode is enabled.

After completing the stage, the player will earn 500 gold coins as an additional reward upon death. The amount can be further increased by an additional 100 coins for each unused revival until the end of the player's run; revivals used beyond that point will still contribute towards the bonus coins.

In the mobile version of the game, if the player did not complete a stage, they may get an option to watch an advertisement to gain one revival. Furthermore, if they get to the results screen, they may get an option to watch an advertisement to earn extra gold.

Interface

[edit | edit source]

The Stage Selection menu becomes accessible after selecting a character in the Character Selection menu and having unlocked Inlaid Library, the second stage of the game (otherwise, a new player would immediately hop onto Mad Forest). The interface displays a list of available stages, each accompanied by its respective information. Players can also activate their unlocked modifiers here before entering a stage.

The following sections provide detailed explanations of the other features visible in the Stage Selection menu.

Stage modifiers

[edit | edit source]

Each stage applies modifiers to certain player and enemy stats. Aside from the stage's base modifiers, extra modifiers can be added by enabling modes. Modifiers can affect the player's movement speed, projectile speed, gold gain, and luck, as well as the enemies' movement speed, health, and quantity. The stage's base modifiers and bonuses conferred by selected modes stack additively, from where the total value of combined bonuses is then applied as a multiplier to the stat boosts from passive items and PowerUps. The Luck bonus is an exception, as it is added to the selected character's Luck stat instead.


Modes and gameplay modifiers

[edit | edit source]

Modes and gameplay modifiers are mechanics that change the way stages are played by adjusting difficulty and adding new features. Modes modify the base stats of both players and enemies, often increasing the challenge of stages while providing additional rewards. Gameplay modifiers, on the other hand, introduce new mechanics or adjust existing ones, allowing players to enhance their performance or alter the dynamics of their runs.

The game includes four unlockable modes: Hyper, Hurry, Inverse, and Endless. Each mode applies specific changes to stage gameplay. Additionally, there are also four unlockable gameplay modifiers: Arcanas, Limit Break, and the Randomizers (which consist of Random Events and Random LevelUp).

Modes and gameplay modifiers can be activated before starting a stage through the Stage Selection menu. Players can enable multiple options at the same time, allowing for combinations of modes and modifiers to create varied gameplay experiences.

List of Modes

[edit | edit source]

Hyper Mode

[edit | edit source]

Hyper Mode increases the character's and enemies' movement speed by 65%–75%, the projectile speed by 15%–25%, and the gold multiplier by 50%. On some stages, hyper mode also provides bonuses to the player's luck stat and enemies' HP. The overall bonuses granted by hyper mode differ with each stage, the values of which are displayed on the Stage Modifiers window.

Unlocking a stage's Hyper Mode varies depending on the stage. Most stages require defeating a specific boss enemy to unlock the hyper mode for that stage, some challenge stages require unlocking a number of hyper modes from other stages in order to unlock their own, while some stages already has hyper mode unlocked for them.

Detailed information regarding the exact values of bonuses conferred by hyper mode for each stage and their unlock requirements is contained in this section.

Hurry Mode

[edit | edit source]

Hurry Mode doubles the stage's Clock Speed and increases XP gain by 25%. The increased Clock Speed causes the in-game timer to move twice as fast, therefore halving the time required to reach a stage's completion in real time. All time-based events (e.g., enemy spawns, evolution chest drops, wave events) occur at their usual timestamps on the in-game timer.

Hurry Mode is unlocked by collecting the  Sorceress' Tears relic, located in Gallo Tower.

Inverse Mode

[edit | edit source]

Inverse Mode increases gold gain by +200% and the Luck bonus by +20%. In addition, enemies start with +200% HP and acquire +5% HP and +0.5 movement speed per minute. This effect is additive with other enemy bonus modifiers from specific stages, such as The Bone Zone and Bat Country.

In Inverse Mode, the stage's layout is rotated by 180°, and the map becomes darker and different in hue. On certain stages, stage items may be placed differently due to the inverted layout. The layout inversion effect can be toggled in the Settings, using the "Visually Invert Stages" option in the Gameplay section.

In this mode, the merchant also offers  Skips,  Banishes,  Rerolls, and an extra  Arcana.

When playing in Inverse Mode on some stages, the stage theme will change to the alternate track if available. However, playing as a character with a unique theme (such as  Pugnala Provola) will override this effect.

Inverse Mode is unlocked by collecting the  Gracia's Mirror relic, located in Eudaimonia Machine.

Endless Mode

[edit | edit source]

Endless Mode disables the spawning of  Reapers upon reaching the stage's time limit. Instead, reaching the final minute of a stage causes the enemy waves to restart from the beginning, completing a "cycle," allowing the stage to continue indefinitely. After completing the first cycle, the stage will be considered complete. Every cycle, enemies gain +100% of their base HP, their spawn frequency and quantity increase by +50%, and their damage dealt increases by +25%; also, the player's max incoming damage cap is reduced by 1.

In this mode, the merchant respawns after each cycle and begins offering one additional Revival in place of  Golden Eggs, as well as an extra Arcana if Inverse Mode is also enabled.

Some characters, like  Megalo Menya Moonspell,  Ghost Lino,  Death and  Megalo Death, still have their run ended when the stage Time Limit is reached even with Endless Mode turned on.

Endless Mode is unlocked by collecting the  Seventh Trumpet relic, located in Eudaimonia Machine.

List of gameplay modifiers

[edit | edit source]

Arcana

[edit | edit source]
Main article: Arcanas

The Arcanas are a set of individual modifiers styled after tarot cards, each with its own set of effects and changes to certain aspects of gameplay.

With Arcanas activated, the player can obtain three of them throughout a run, the first of which is given at the start of the run and the other two from Arcana chests dropped by special bosses at specific timestamps. Although only three Arcanas can normally be possessed simultaneously, the player can have an unlimited number of Arcanas. Having more than three Arcanas is possible by playing as certain characters who can obtain additional Arcanas (such as Santa), starts with an extra Arcana (such as Avatar), playing with Inverse Mode on, or using the Moonlight Bolero (VI) Darkana.

The Arcana system is unlocked by collecting the Randomazzo relic located in Gallo Tower, along with the first Arcana for the player, Sarabande of Healing (VI). Collecting the Darkasso in Room 1665 expands the system to incorporate Darkanas, which increases the selection of available Arcana. Each Arcana and Darkana can be unlocked by completing their corresponding achievements.

Limit Break

[edit | edit source]
Main article: Limit Break

Limit Break enables equipped weapons to be upgraded beyond their usual limits, allowing weapons to become more powerful than what they normally are.

With Limit Break activated, upon maxing out all equipped weapon and passive items, the player will no longer receive Coin or Floor Chicken options. Instead, they will start to receive Limit Break upgrades, with each level-up providing an upgrade to a specific stat for one of the player's equipped weapons.

Weapons with Limit Break levels may still be evolved. However, the weapon will lose all of its additionally obtained stats and its level will start back from 1. Certain weapons are excluded from this rule such as SpellStrom and Alucard Shield , which keeps the Limit Break stats of their pre-evolutions even after evolving.

Limit Break is unlocked by collecting the Great Gospel relic after defeating The Ender in Cappella Magna.

Randomizers

[edit | edit source]

Randomizers are special gameplay options which adds a factor of unpredictability to the gameplay, as their name implies. Unlike other modifiers which is intended to directly enhance gameplay, Randomizers focus on increasing stage difficulty by incorporating an element of randomness to each run.

Currently, the game features two Randomizers: Random Events and Random LevelUp. These options can be accessed through a dedicated window in the Stage Selection screen, located alongside the windows for modes and other gameplay modifiers. Notably, this Randomizers window is not visible initially and only becomes available after unlocking at least one Randomizer.

Random Events
[edit | edit source]
Main article: Random Events

Random Events, also referred to as Randomized Minute Events, introduce a spinning roulette to the bottom-right corner of the screen when activated before a run, which appears as The Directer's masks on three black concentric rings. At the 30-second mark of every minute, the roulette selects a random event, which then triggers at the start of the following minute. These events vary widely in their effects—some may be beneficial, others detrimental, and some simply add a touch of humor without significant impact.

The Random Events option is unlocked by collecting the Trisection relic, located in Astral Stair. Notably, this feature is always enabled by default in Astral Stair before acquiring the Trisection relic. Once unlocked, it can be toggled on or off like other stage modifiers.

Random LevelUp
[edit | edit source]

Random LevelUp, when enabled before a run, removes the player’s ability to manually select items during level-ups. Instead, the game automatically designates a random weapon or passive item to be added or upgraded in the player’s inventory. If Limit Break is also enabled, Random LevelUp will continue to apply its effects to it as well.

The Random LevelUp option is unlocked by collecting the Brave Story relic, which appears in Space 54 at the 18-minute mark.

Music selection

[edit | edit source]
See also: Music

Music Selection is a feature that allows players to customize the background music of a stage by choosing from any unlocked music tracks in the game. Players can also apply modifiers to the music, which include Normal, Hyper, and Forsaken. The Normal modifier plays the track in its original form, while Hyper increases the speed and pitch of the music, and conversely, Forsaken slows the music down and lowers its pitch.

The Music Selection menu is unlocked by collecting the Magic Banger relic, located in Green Acres.

    • beta  16 April 2026 (Update):

    • beta  15 January 2026 (Update):
      • Fixed stage specific achievements incorrectly unlocking for newly unlocked stages if their conditions were met during the run where you unlocked the stage
    • update 1 December 2025 (Update):
      • Stage selection will revert to mad forest if selected stage is from an unavailable DLC in online
    • update 17 August 2023 (Update):
      • Fixed a situation where invalid stage data could cause a crash.
      • Corrected gameplay zoom level to better match the original engine.
      • Stage Select: Tweaked font size/alignment.
      • On Stage Complete:
        • Updated button animations.
        • Updated text animations.
        • Updated revive animations.
        • Updated timings.
    • update 30 October 2022 (Update):
      • fix for BGM selection getting locked when entering special menus
      • Fixed stage modifiers to work with the new system
      • fixed controller input in Stage Selection
    • update 20 October 2022 (Update):
      • Added Inverse mode
        • Stages layout is upside down (can be disabled in Options) plus minor visual changes depending on stage.
        • Gold gains are increased by 200%. Luck bonus of 20%.
        • Enemies start with +200% Max Health.
        • Enemies gain 5% Max Health every minute and and 1% movement speed every 2 minutes (cumulative with the Bone Zone enemy bonuses).
        • The merchant also sells: Skip, Banish, and Reroll (can carry up to 20) and 1 extra Arcana.
      • Added Endless mode
        • The Reaper won't spawn at the final minute.
        • Reaching the final minute of a stage will make the enemy waves to restart from minute 0, completing a "cycle".
        • Enemies gain 100% of their base Max Health per cycle.
        • Enemies spawn frequency and amount is increased by 50% per cycle.
        • Enemies deal 25% more damage per cycle. The player's max damage cap is diminished by 1 per cycle.
        • The merchant respawns on every cycle and sells "+1 Revival" instead of Golden Eggs.
      • New UI to show what relics are available in each stage
    • update 2 September 2022 (Update):
      • Option to cash-in immediately all remaining Revives when completing a stage.
    • update 23 June 2022 (Update):
      • Hurry mode also gives a 25% XP bonus
    • update 9 June 2022 (Update):
      • The Stage Selection screen will now display when the lunar eclipse event is available in Moongolow (required to unlock Stage 5)
    • update 23 May 2022 (Update):
      • Music and music mods can now be previewd in stage selection screen
    • update 24 April 2022 (Update):
      • Added a toggle in Stage Selection to disable Arcanas. Disabling them also disables the extra minibosses at 11:00 and 21:00
    • update 12 April 2022 (Update):
      • Minor changes to spawn waves
      • Specified Clock Speed in Stage Selection
    • update 5 April 2022 (Update):
      • Unlocked by finding the new relic in Stage 4, the new "Hurry" option in Stage Selection will simply make the timer tick twice as fast, so enemies waves will change every 30 seconds in real time instead of the usual 60.
    • update 25 March 2022 (Update):
      • Added stage modifiers information on the Stage Selection screen
    • update 12 March 2022 (Update):
      • special stage pickups are now immune to bad/purple Pentagram, but now Pentagram also correctly erases all other on-screen pickups
      • tentative fix for UI elements overlapping when pickipng up a stage item at the same time as leveling up or pausing the game
    • update 2 March 2022 (Update):
      • hidden stage items now have a 100% chance to appear
    • update 2 January 2022 (Update):
      • added support for tileset maps

      • added a coins reward for reaching the end of the stage
