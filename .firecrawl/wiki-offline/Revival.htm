Revival

Revival is one of the player stats in Vampire Survivors. Each point of Revival a player has allows them to revive once upon death with 50% of their  Max Health.

The player has 0 base Revivals before modifications. Bonuses to Revival are flat bonuses and thus stack additively.

There is no upper limit to how many Revivals the player may have.

Effects

[edit | edit source]

If the player dies and has at least 1 Revival, instead of getting the usual "Quit" button to return to the main menu, they will instead be greeted with a "Revive" button. An animation is played and the player resumes control of their character exactly where they left off, with 50% of their  Max Health also being restored. The player is additionally given a large amount of invulnerability frames. The health gained upon reviving counts as healing, causing it to interact with effects related to restoring health, such as  Soul Eater and  Sarabande of Healing (VI).

If Revivals are carried to the end of the game, the player will still be allowed to Revive after being killed by  The Reaper. Every Revival used like this will add gold to the bonus gold for completing the stage, at +100 gold per revival, increasing by 100 for every additional revival used. For example, if the player has 3 revivals and use them all on The Reaper, they will die and get the normal 500 bonus gold, then revive and die and get an extra 100, then an extra 200, then an extra 300, and end the round with 1100 bonus gold. The scaling gold increase caps at 400 and any additional unused Revivals will only give 100 bonus gold.

Revival also affects:

  • Base Damage and Amount of  Phieraggi
  • Base Damage of  Meal Ticket

From items

[edit | edit source]
  • Revival's associated passive item is  Tirajisú, which provides 1 additional revival per level, up to a maximum of 2 at level 2.
  •  Golden Eggs can permanently increase Revival by 0.1 for the character that collected it.
  • Survarot cards featuring  Pasqualina Belpaese provide +1 Revival upon selection. The card's edition-exclusive skill (or any edition card as a random bonus) can grant up to +3 Revivals after reaching certain kill thresholds.
  •  Academy Badge  has 6 levels and provides +1 Revival at levels 3 and 5, for a maximum of +2 Revival.
  •  Paranormal Scan  may provide 0.2 Revival whenever it activates, requiring 5 such bonuses to give +1 Revival, with no cap.
  •  Weapon Power-Up  provides +1 Revival on pickup.


From arcana

[edit | edit source]
  •  Awake (IV) provides +3 Revivals when chosen and gives +10%  Max Health, +1  Armor, and +5%  Might,  Area,  Duration, and  Speed per Revival consumed.
  •  Hidden Anathema (III) may provide additional Revivals when obtaining food-based Pickups, with no cap.

Base Game

[edit | edit source]
  •  Krochi Freetto starts with +1 Revival and gains +1 Revival at level 33, for a total of +2 Revivals.
  •  Cosmo Pavone starts with +1 Revival and gains +1 Revival every 100 levels, with no cap.
  •  Gyorunton and  Bats Bats Bats both start with +2 Revivals.
  •  Queen Sigma and  Avatar Infernas both start with +1 Revival.
  •  Gazebo may gain additional Revivals when Overhealing, up to a cap of +3 Revivals.
  •  Reborn She-Moon Eeta starts with +7 Revivals.

Emergency Meeting

  •  Guardian Pina starts with +1 Revival, and has a chance to gain +1 Revival when anyone, including Pina themself, revives.

Operation Guns

  •  Nathan Graves and  Chaos both start with  Awake (IV) and thus start with +3 Revivals.

[edit | edit source]
  •  Dolores starts with +1 Revival.
  •  Lolo, Hiss, Meow, and Purr start with +8 Revivals.
  •  Kugutsu gain +1 Revival and +5%  Growth every time they Glimmer a new tech, up to a maximum of +35 Revivals (and +175% Growth) should they collect and Glimmer all compatible weaponry.

Tips

[edit | edit source]
  • Revivals are a powerful tool that can save runs. While they provide no benefit until you die (except for Phieraggi), they can save the player from sloppy mistakes and being suddenly overwhelmed.
  • Phieraggi benefits from this stat by getting +X Base Damage and +X Amount equal to the value of this stat. Be sure not to use up revives to achive that weapons maximum effectiveness.
  • Revivals are especially useful when playing as characters with less survivability, such as Exdash and Toastie.
  • Revival stat is additive. The maximum amount of Revivals a player can have can be obtained by getting the PowerUp (+1), Level 2 Tirajisú (+2), playing as Krochi or Gyorunton (+2), and taking the Awake (IV) Arcana (+3), netting +8 extra lives. If these are taken to the end of the round and exchanged with The Reaper for extra gold, they will be worth +1900 bonus gold altogether.
  • Revivals can be especially useful for runs where the goal is to survive as long as possible against The Reaper, and can greatly extend runs when paired with other defensive measures.
  • Revivals can be useful for gathering loot otherwise unobtainable before The Reaper ends the run. Even 1 revival can allow the player to get a few more levels or one extra Treasure Chest.
