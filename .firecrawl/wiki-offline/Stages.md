**Stages** are the playing fields where the player fights off hordes of [enemies](Enemy.md) to survive as long as possible until the stage\'s time limit.

The game currently features 80 official stages, divided into 33 primary stages and 47 [adventure mode](Adventures.md)-exclusive stages. The primary stages include 20 stages from the base game, consisting of 5 normal stages, 7 bonus stages, 9 challenge stages, 2 special stages, and 1 hidden stage, as well as 9 additional stages from the DLCs. Adventure mode, on the other hand, includes 13 stages from the base game and 34 stages from all DLC adventures combined (not counting the identical repeating stages of the main game). Stages are unlocked by completing specific requirements tied to their corresponding achievements, without the need to be bought with [coins](Gold_Coin_(currency).md). The exception is [Mad Forest](Mad_Forest.md), which is the first playable stage and is unlocked by default.

## Overview {#Overview}

**Stages** are the primary areas where players fight against enemy waves, with the goal of surviving until the time limit ends, which is at 30:00, 20:00, or 15:00 depending on the stage.

Stages differ in their layout and structure. Some, such as [Mad Forest](Mad_Forest.md), [Green Acres](Green_Acres.md), and [The Bone Zone](The_Bone_Zone.md), feature open areas that allow players to move freely. Others, like [Inlaid Library](Inlaid_Library.md), [Gallo Tower](Gallo_Tower.md), and [Tiny Bridge](Tiny_Bridge.md), have more confined designs, restricting movement to specific directions. Stages also vary in their environmental features, with some containing obstacles, walls, or corridors that require players to navigate carefully. Certain stages include interactive elements, such as *trap pizzas*, which are pressure plates that trigger [map events](Map_event.md) when activated, or the teleporting doors in [Astral Stair](Astral_Stair.md), which lead to rooms accessible only through a specific door.

Players can interact with various objects found in stages. **Destructibles** are common and can be broken by hitting them with the player's weapons. The most frequently encountered destructible is the [light source](Light_source.md), which appears in all stages and drops a random [pickup item](Pickups.md) when destroyed. Some stages also contain unique destructibles, such as the [Dairy Carts](Dairy_Cart.md) in Dairy Plant or the levers in [Laborratory](Laborratory.md). Additionally, **stage items** are pre-placed objects that players can collect, including [weapons](Weapons.md), [passive items](Passive_items.md), [pickups](Pickups.md), and [relics](Relics.md). Other notable features are the [**coffins**](Coffin.md), which are found in certain stages and can be opened to unlock new characters, and the [Merchant](Merchant.md), which allows the player to purchase rare weapons, power-ups, [Golden Eggs](Golden_Egg.md), and more.

### Completing a stage {#Completing_a_stage}

<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb">

<figcaption>Example of a "Stage Completed" screen in <a href="Moongolow.md">Moongolow</a>.</figcaption>
</figure>

If the player survives until the stage\'s time limit, the stage is considered complete, at which point one [Reaper](The_Reaper.md) will arrive every minute to slay the player and end their run, unless [Endless mode](#Endless_Mode) is enabled.

After completing the stage, the player will earn 500 [gold coins](Gold_Coin_(currency).md) as an additional reward upon death. The amount can be further increased by an additional 100 coins for each unused [revival](Revival.md) until the end of the player\'s run; revivals used beyond that point will still contribute towards the bonus coins.

In the mobile version of the game, if the player did not complete a stage, they may get an option to watch an advertisement to gain one [revival](Revival.md). Furthermore, if they get to the [results](Results.md) screen, they may get an option to watch an advertisement to earn extra gold.

## Interface {#Interface}

<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb">

<figcaption>Stage Selection menu</figcaption>
</figure>

The **Stage Selection menu** becomes accessible after selecting a [character](Characters.md) in the Character Selection menu and having unlocked [Inlaid Library](Inlaid_Library.md), the second stage of the game (otherwise, a new player would immediately hop onto [Mad Forest](Mad_Forest.md)). The interface displays a list of available stages, each accompanied by its respective information. Players can also activate their unlocked modifiers here before entering a stage.

The following sections provide detailed explanations of the other features visible in the Stage Selection menu.\

### Stage modifiers {#Stage_modifiers}

<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Frameless">

<figcaption>Stage modifiers window</figcaption>
</figure>

Each stage applies modifiers to certain player and enemy stats. Aside from the stage\'s base modifiers, extra modifiers can be added by enabling [modes](#Modes_and_gameplay_modifiers). Modifiers can affect the player\'s [movement speed](Move_Speed.md), [projectile speed](Speed.md), [gold gain](Greed.md), and [luck](Luck.md), as well as the [enemies](Enemies.md)\' movement speed, health, and quantity. The stage\'s base modifiers and bonuses conferred by selected modes stack additively, from where the total value of combined bonuses is then applied as a multiplier to the stat boosts from [passive items](Passive_items.md) and [PowerUps](PowerUps.md). The Luck bonus is an exception, as it is added to the selected character\'s Luck stat instead.

### Modes and gameplay modifiers {#Modes_and_gameplay_modifiers}

<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb">

<figcaption>Modes and gameplay modifiers window</figcaption>
</figure>

**Modes** and **gameplay modifiers** are mechanics that change the way stages are played by adjusting difficulty and adding new features. Modes modify the base stats of both players and enemies, often increasing the challenge of stages while providing additional rewards. Gameplay modifiers, on the other hand, introduce new mechanics or adjust existing ones, allowing players to enhance their performance or alter the dynamics of their runs.

The game includes four unlockable modes: **Hyper**, **Hurry**, **Inverse**, and **Endless**. Each mode applies specific changes to stage gameplay. Additionally, there are also four unlockable gameplay modifiers: **Arcanas**, **Limit Break**, and the **Randomizers** (which consist of *Random Events* and *Random LevelUp*).

Modes and gameplay modifiers can be activated before starting a stage through the **Stage Selection menu**. Players can enable multiple options at the same time, allowing for combinations of modes and modifiers to create varied gameplay experiences.

### List of Modes {#List_of_Modes}

#### Hyper Mode {#Hyper_Mode}

**Hyper Mode** increases the character\'s and enemies\' movement speed by 65%--75%, the projectile speed by 15%--25%, and the gold multiplier by 50%. On some stages, hyper mode also provides bonuses to the player\'s luck stat and enemies\' HP. The overall bonuses granted by hyper mode differ with each stage, the values of which are displayed on the Stage Modifiers window.

Unlocking a stage\'s Hyper Mode varies depending on the stage. Most stages require defeating a specific boss enemy to unlock the hyper mode for that stage, some challenge stages require unlocking a number of hyper modes from other stages in order to unlock their own, while some stages already has hyper mode unlocked for them.

*Detailed information regarding the exact values of bonuses conferred by hyper mode for each stage and their unlock requirements is contained in [this section](#Available_stages).*

#### Hurry Mode {#Hurry_Mode}

**Hurry Mode** doubles the stage\'s Clock Speed and increases [XP gain](Growth.md) by 25%. The increased Clock Speed causes the in-game timer to move twice as fast, therefore halving the time required to reach a stage\'s completion in real time. All time-based events (e.g., enemy spawns, [evolution](Evolution.md) chest drops, [wave events](Enemies.md#Map_events)) occur at their usual timestamps on the in-game timer.

Hurry Mode is unlocked by collecting the  [Sorceress\' Tears](Sorceress'_Tears.md) relic, located in [Gallo Tower](Gallo_Tower.md).

#### Inverse Mode {#Inverse_Mode}

**Inverse Mode** increases gold gain by +200% and the Luck bonus by +20%. In addition, enemies start with +200% HP and acquire +5% HP and +0.5 movement speed per minute. This effect is additive with other enemy bonus modifiers from specific stages, such as [The Bone Zone](The_Bone_Zone.md) and [Bat Country](Bat_Country.md).

In Inverse Mode, the stage\'s layout is rotated by 180°, and the map becomes darker and different in hue. On certain stages, stage items may be placed differently due to the inverted layout. The layout inversion effect can be toggled in the Settings, using the \"*Visually Invert Stages\"* option in the Gameplay section.

In this mode, the [merchant](Merchant.md) also offers  [Skips](Skip.md),  [Banishes](Banish.md),  [Rerolls](Reroll.md), and an extra  [Arcana](Arcanas.md).

When playing in Inverse Mode on some stages, the stage [theme](Music.md) will change to the alternate track if available. However, playing as a character with a unique theme (such as  [Pugnala Provola](Pugnala_Provola.md)) will override this effect.

Inverse Mode is unlocked by collecting the  [Gracia\'s Mirror](Gracia's_Mirror.md) relic, located in [Eudaimonia Machine](Eudaimonia_Machine.md).

#### Endless Mode {#Endless_Mode}

**Endless Mode** disables the spawning of  [Reapers](The_Reaper.md) upon reaching the stage\'s time limit. Instead, reaching the final minute of a stage causes the enemy waves to restart from the beginning, completing a \"cycle,\" allowing the stage to continue indefinitely. After completing the first cycle, the stage will be considered complete. Every cycle, enemies gain +100% of their base HP, their spawn frequency and quantity increase by +50%, and their damage dealt increases by +25%; also, the player\'s max incoming damage cap is reduced by 1.

In this mode, the [merchant](Merchant.md) respawns after each cycle and begins offering one additional [Revival](Revival.md) in place of  [Golden Eggs](Golden_Egg.md), as well as an extra Arcana if Inverse Mode is also enabled.

Some characters, like  [Megalo Menya Moonspell](Megalo_Menya_Moonspell.md),  [Ghost Lino](Ghost_Lino.md),  [Death](Death_(character).md) and  [Megalo Death](Megalo_Death.md), still have their run ended when the stage Time Limit is reached even with Endless Mode turned on.

Endless Mode is unlocked by collecting the  [Seventh Trumpet](Seventh_Trumpet.md) relic, located in [Eudaimonia Machine](Eudaimonia_Machine.md).

### List of gameplay modifiers {#List_of_gameplay_modifiers}

#### Arcana {#Arcana}

Main article: [Arcanas](Arcanas.md)

The **Arcanas** are a set of individual modifiers styled after tarot cards, each with its own set of effects and changes to certain aspects of gameplay.

With Arcanas activated, the player can obtain three of them throughout a run, the first of which is given at the start of the run and the other two from [Arcana chests](Treasure_Chest.md#Arcana) dropped by special bosses at specific timestamps. Although only three Arcanas can normally be possessed simultaneously, the player can have an unlimited number of Arcanas. Having more than three Arcanas is possible by playing as certain characters who can obtain additional Arcanas (such as [Santa](Santa.md)), starts with an extra Arcana (such as [Avatar](Avatar.md)), playing with Inverse Mode on, or using the [Moonlight Bolero (VI)](Moonlight_Bolero_(VI).md) Darkana.

The Arcana system is unlocked by collecting the [Randomazzo](Randomazzo.md) relic located in [Gallo Tower](Gallo_Tower.md), along with the first Arcana for the player, [Sarabande of Healing (VI)](Sarabande_of_Healing_(VI).md). Collecting the [Darkasso](Darkasso.md) in [Room 1665](Room_1665.md) expands the system to incorporate **Darkanas**, which increases the selection of available Arcana. Each Arcana and Darkana can be unlocked by completing their corresponding achievements.

#### Limit Break {#Limit_Break}

Main article: [Limit Break](Limit_Break.md)

**Limit Break** enables equipped weapons to be upgraded beyond their usual limits, allowing weapons to become more powerful than what they normally are.

With Limit Break activated, upon maxing out all equipped weapon and passive items, the player will no longer receive [Coin](Gold_Coin_(currency).md) or [Floor Chicken](Floor_Chicken.md) options. Instead, they will start to receive Limit Break upgrades, with each [level-up](Level_up.md) providing an upgrade to a specific stat for one of the player\'s equipped weapons.

Weapons with Limit Break levels may still be evolved. However, the weapon will lose all of its additionally obtained stats and its level will start back from 1. Certain weapons are excluded from this rule such as [SpellStrom](SpellStrom.md) and [Alucard Shield](Alucard_Shield.md) , which keeps the Limit Break stats of their pre-evolutions even after evolving.

Limit Break is unlocked by collecting the [Great Gospel](Great_Gospel.md) relic after defeating The Ender in [Cappella Magna](Cappella_Magna.md).

#### Randomizers {#Randomizers}

<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Frameless">

<figcaption>Randomizers window</figcaption>
</figure>

**Randomizers** are special gameplay options which adds a factor of unpredictability to the gameplay, as their name implies. Unlike other modifiers which is intended to directly enhance gameplay, Randomizers focus on increasing stage difficulty by incorporating an element of randomness to each run.

Currently, the game features two Randomizers: *Random Events* and *Random LevelUp*. These options can be accessed through a dedicated window in the Stage Selection screen, located alongside the windows for modes and other gameplay modifiers. Notably, this Randomizers window is not visible initially and only becomes available after unlocking at least one Randomizer.

##### Random Events {#Random_Events}

Main article: [Random Events](Random_Events.md)

**Random Events**, also referred to as Randomized Minute Events, introduce a spinning roulette to the bottom-right corner of the screen when activated before a run, which appears as [The Directer](The_Directer.md)\'s masks on three black concentric rings. At the 30-second mark of every minute, the roulette selects a random event, which then triggers at the start of the following minute. These events vary widely in their effects---some may be beneficial, others detrimental, and some simply add a touch of humor without significant impact.

The Random Events option is unlocked by collecting the [Trisection](Trisection.md) relic, located in [Astral Stair](Astral_Stair.md). Notably, this feature is always enabled by default in Astral Stair before acquiring the Trisection relic. Once unlocked, it can be toggled on or off like other stage modifiers.

##### Random LevelUp {#Random_LevelUp}

**Random LevelUp**, when enabled before a run, removes the player's ability to manually select items during level-ups. Instead, the game automatically designates a random weapon or passive item to be added or upgraded in the player's inventory. If Limit Break is also enabled, Random LevelUp will continue to apply its effects to it as well.

The Random LevelUp option is unlocked by collecting the [Brave Story](Brave_Story.md) relic, which appears in [Space 54](Space_54.md) at the 18-minute mark.

### Music selection {#Music_selection}

See also: [Music](Music.md)

<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Frameless">

</figure>

**Music Selection** is a feature that allows players to customize the [background music](Music.md) of a stage by choosing from any unlocked music tracks in the game. Players can also apply modifiers to the music, which include **Normal**, **Hyper**, and **Forsaken**. The *Normal* modifier plays the track in its original form, while *Hyper* increases the speed and pitch of the music, and conversely, *Forsaken* slows the music down and lowers its pitch.

The Music Selection menu is unlocked by collecting the [Magic Banger](Magic_Banger.md) relic, located in [Green Acres](Green_Acres.md).\
