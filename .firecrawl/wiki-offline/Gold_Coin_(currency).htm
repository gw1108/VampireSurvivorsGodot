Gold Coin (currency)

From Vampire Survivors Wiki
Jump to navigation Jump to search
This article is about the currency. For the pickup, see Gold Coin (pickup).

Gold Coins are the basic currency in the game and are used for purchasing PowerUps, new characters, and items from  Merchant.

The collected amount of gold coins is depicted on the top center area of the game's interface, particularly when the player is currently not playing a round.

Obtaining

[edit | edit source]

Gold Coins can be obtained as pickups in the form of  Gold Coin,  Coin Bag or  Rich Coin Bag from destroyed light sources. Opening Treasure Chests will always give a random amount of Gold Coins between 60-500. If none of the items in the player's inventory can be upgraded, new Chests will award extra coins instead, and once the inventory is full and maxed out, level ups will give the player an option for extra Coins. Gold Coins are also awarded when the player finishes the run just after being killed by  The Reaper.

The amount of Gold Coins earned can be increased with  Greed stat, which can be gained from the PowerUp of the same name (up to +50%) and the  Stone Mask (up to +50%) passive item. Additionally, the Hyper mode's modifiers in any stage further increase the amount of gold gained (x1.5 - x2 multiplier). With aforementioned upgrades, the player can gain up to 4x the original amount of coins.

Gold Coins have a visual cap of 9,999,999. The true amount of gold obtained is only visible in game and in the results screen. The internal amount of Gold Coins is hard capped at 3.402823 E+38, which is the 32-bit floating point maximum.

Adventures

[edit | edit source]

In Adventures mode, the amount of gold coins collected in each adventure is independent from that of the main game and other adventures.

A key feature in Adventures is Planar Bazaar, a place where players can spend coins to unlock the availability of particular weapons and passives in their current adventure. These weapons and passives are offered by various characters who serve as merchants in the adventure.

If a player spends their coins on unlocking or buying a weapon or passive item in a different stage, they will have that item equipped for that stage.
