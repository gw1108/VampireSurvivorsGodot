Merchant

From Vampire Survivors Wiki
Jump to navigation Jump to search
For the playable character, see Big Trouser.

The Merchant is a mechanic in Vampire Survivors, which allows the purchasing of rare Weapons and Golden Eggs using Gold Coins. They initially only appear in Moongolow. Then later in all other stages after the purchase of the Glass Vizard.

Wares

[edit | edit source]

The Merchant offers the following:

Items Type Description Cost Note
 Pick a Card Buff Gives an extra Arcana for this run. 20,000 Inverse mode only.
 Skip Buff Gives +1 Skip for this run. 100 Inverse mode only. Can be purchased repeatedly until the player has 20 Skips. Can only buy once if the character already start with 20 Skips.
 Banish Buff Gives +1 Banish for this run. 500 Inverse mode only. Can be purchased repeatedly until the player has 20 Banishes. Can only buy once if the character already start with 20 Banishes.
 Reroll Buff Gives +1 Reroll for this run. 1,000 Inverse mode only. Can be purchased repeatedly until the player has 20 Rerolls. Can only buy once if the character already start with 20 Rerolls.
 Golden Egg Buff Permanently improves a random stat for the current character, by a tiny amount. 10,000 Can be purchased repeatedly. Not available for  Queen Sigma.
 Candybox Weapon Allows you to choose any unlocked base weapon. 1,000 Only available for Queen Sigma.
 Glass Vizard Relic Summons the merchant in all stages. 10,000 Sold in Moongolow only. One-time purchase.
 Bone Weapon Throws a bouncing projectile. 1,000
 Cherry Bomb Weapon Throws a bouncing projectile that explodes after some time. 1,000
 Carréllo Weapon Throws a bouncing projectile. Number of bounces affected by Amount. 1,000
 Celestial Dusting Weapon Throws a bouncing projectile. Cooldown reduces when moving. 1,000

Other Merchants

[edit | edit source]

The Merchant is not the only vendor in the game.

  • The  Master Librarian from Ode to Castlevania, who allows purchasing of various Ode to Castlevania weapons.
  • Adventure Merchants, accessible inside of Adventures, especially inside the Planar Bazaar stage. The Adventure Merchants sell a plethora of items and unlock them for the Adventure the player is currently in.
    • All of the Merchants in Planar Bazaar also happen to be player characters as well, except for  Xanthia, who can also be seen selling the  Emerald Disk and the  Joker Disk in Moongolow.
  • The  Eggman in Moongolow, who allows for bulk purchasing of Golden Eggs.
  • The  Healer in Moongolow, who allows for the removal of Golden Eggs.
  •  Giocare, who appears in Westwoods, sells spins for the Spin the Wheel! minigame.
  •  Big Troubler, who appears at the top of The Lycaeum and sells the  Green Eyes Vizard,  Survarocchi, and  Enemahs.

Notes

[edit | edit source]
  • In order to access the shop, the player needs to touch the merchant.
  • Golden Egg applies to the current character being played but its effects will not apply until after the current game ends.
  • All rare weapons (Bone, Cherry Bomb, Carréllo, Celestial Dusting, La Robba) bought lasts for the current game and cannot be bought if the max weapon limit is already reached.
  • The player is unable to purchase weapons if the player's weapon slots are full already.
  • The merchant can only be accessed once per run. Once the player exits the shop, the merchant disappears from the map.
    • The merchant can be accessed infinite times in Endless Mode, spawning every cycle.
  • The merchant does not disappear after a certain time. They will remain like this until the player interacts with them.

Trivia

[edit | edit source]
  • The merchant has a particle effect behind their head, similar with the head/masks of the Masked Guardians that guard the four secret Passive items.
  • Because of Gold Fever and Vicious Hunger which generate an abundant number of coins, the merchant is added to provide Coin sink after the player finishes spending all their coins on upgrades and unlocking new characters.
  • The merchant's appearance is based on the character Pantalone from Italian commedia dell'arte.
  • In Carlo Cart, the merchant runs on the top of the track at the same speed as the passive movement. The player can get to him by pressing right.
  • The phrase, "Interested in this?" at the top of his menu is a nod to Alucard's interaction with the Librarian in Symphony of the Night, where he will tell the shopkeeper, "I'm interested in this," upon browsing the inventory.
