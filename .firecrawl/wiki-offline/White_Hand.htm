The White Hand is a special event that takes place once the player has either defeated  The Reaper after damaging it with the  Infinite Corridor or  Crimson Shroud, or immediately after the player has defeated certain major stage bosses which are as follows:

  •  The Ender
  •  Avatar Infernas
  •  Je-Ne-Viv 
  •  Big Fuzz 

It may also spawn during the battle with  The Directer if the player has any Revivals.

Behavior

[edit | edit source]

When the White Hand event is triggered, the screen will start to turn red, the camera will slowly zoom in towards the player's character, and the sound of a church bell strikes twelve times. After the twelfth strike, the White Hand appears from the left side of the screen and will start to slowly approach the player. The White Hand's movement is relative to the player's view rather than the character's position on the map. This means that it is not possible to outrun the White Hand.

Once the White Hand has reached the player, the player will instantly die. Effects that grant invulnerability or intangibility do not work against the White Hand, because rather than dealing damage, a hardcoded event triggers when the White Hand reachers the player which sets their current health to 0, ignoring any form of invincibility. It is impossible to kill or stall the White Hand, and any time the player attempts to revive, the White Hand will kill them very shortly after.

The White Hand's behavior is identical when triggered any way except for the battle with The Directer in Eudaimonia Machine.

During the battle with The Directer, the White Hand may be dragged from the right side onto the screen by one of The Directer's hands to kill the player. This can happen multiple times. If the event triggers and the player has no Revivals, they are still killed by the White Hand, but are revived automatically with a special animation.

If the player has additional speed from the  Roast Chicken with a Clock in the Middle, its effect will be disabled upon the White Hand event.

Trivia

[edit | edit source]
  • Due to the Crimson Shroud and Infinite Corridor's abilities to kill The Reaper easily, this event was added as a way to bring the current run to a close.
  • The name could be a reference to the Pokémon creepypasta sprite "White Hand" of the same name.
  • The twelve bell tolls before the White Hand's arrival are known as a death knell, done to signify someone's death or an impending death, which may reference either the Red Death dying itself, or the player's impending death.
  • The game does not detect whether the White Hand has touched the player as it is a sprite, instead the kill event is triggered once the White Hand reaches the center of the screen.
  • While the screen zooms in during each toll, pausing the screen zooms the game back out. This does not change the end result of the cutscene, however.
