Cooldown

From Vampire Survivors Wiki
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Cooldown is one of the player stats in Vampire Survivors. It affects the length of the delay between attacks done by most weapons.

The player has 100% base Cooldown before modifications, such as the characters bonuses or PowerUps. The Cooldown in the stat panel is given as the difference to the base Cooldown, meaning that a Cooldown stat of -30% equals to 70% total Cooldown, for example. Cooldown modifications stack additively.

The lower limit of total Cooldown is 10%.

 Browny  gains +2%  Area per -1% Cooldown.

The Cooldown PowerUp decreases Cooldown by 2.5% per rank, with a maximum rank of 2. At rank 2, it provides -5% Cooldown. It initially costs 900.

From items

[edit | edit source]

Cooldown's associated passive item is the  Empty Tome, which reduces Cooldown by -8% per level, down to a maximum of -40% Cooldown at level 5.

  •  Gold Finger provides -100% Cooldown for 10 seconds ( Hail from the Future (X) helps with getting these often).
  •  Rapid Fire  provides -30% Cooldown for 10 seconds, stacking up to -100% total character Cooldown.
  •  Centralis Custos  provides -10% Cooldown when the player character takes damage from enemies.
  •  Trinum Custodem  provides a passive -10% Cooldown and an additional -10% Cooldown when the player character takes damage from enemies.
  •  Paranormal Scan  has a chance of granting a Cooldown stat bonus of -3% upon activation.
  •  Golden Egg can permanently reduce Cooldown by -0.5% for the character that collected it.

From arcana

[edit | edit source]
  •  Tragic Princess (III) decreases Cooldown when moving. It is not limited by the -90% Cooldown cap.
  •  Silent Old Sanctuary (XX) provides -8% Cooldown when chosen per active weapon slot left empty, for a maximum of -40% Cooldown

From characters

Base Game

[edit | edit source]
  •  Arca reduces Cooldown by -5% every 5 levels, up to -15% at level 15.
  •  Porta starts with -90% Cooldown, every level increases by +30% until it returns to normal by level 4.
  •  Christine and  Sigma start with -25% Cooldown.
  •  Gallo starts with -15% Cooldown.
  •  Exdash and  Toastie both start with +10% Cooldown.
  •  Smith starts with +10% Cooldown and reduces Cooldown by -0.25% every level, with no cap.
  •  Avatar reduces Cooldown by -0.25% every level, with no cap.
  •  Minnah's Cooldown cycles between multiplied by 2 and divided by 2 every minute.
  •  Leda starts with -10% Cooldown.
  •  Santa starts with -70 Cooldown and increases by 20% at levels 2 and 3 until she has -10% Cooldown.

Legacy of the Moonspell

[edit | edit source]
  •  Menya starts with -10% Cooldown. She gains -20% Cooldown in her boosted state.
  •  Babi-Onna is not affected by Cooldown. She attacks by posing and its rate is increased by  Move Speed.
  •  Megalo Menya starts with -20% Cooldown.

Tides of the Foscari

[edit | edit source]
  •  Keitha starts with -10% Cooldown.
  •  Luminaire gains -100% Cooldown temporarily after reviving.

Emergency Meeting

[edit | edit source]
  •  Crewmate has a chance to lose a random amount of Cooldown every time a weapon or passive item is upgraded to its maximum level, with no cap.
  •  Impostor gains -20% Cooldown while in their boosted state.
  •  Megalo Impostor starts with -10% Cooldown.

Operation Guns

[edit | edit source]
  •  Lance gains -1% Cooldown at specific kill thresholds up to 25%.
  •  Lucia gains -3% Cooldown after reviving, with no cap.
  •  Brad gains up to -50% Cooldown based on missing percentage of  Max Health scaling linearly.
  •  Sheena gains -1% Cooldown per 2%  Move Speed.

Ode to Castlevania

[edit | edit source]
  •  Mina,  Eric and  Nathan start with -5% Cooldown.
  •  Richter and  Succubus start with -10% Cooldown.
  •  Megalo Elizabeth,  Megalo Olrox,  Megalo Death and  Megalo Dracula start with -20% Cooldown.
  •  Saint Germain reduces Cooldown by 0.2% every level, with no cap.
  •  Leon reduces Cooldown by 5% upon obtaining the  Vampire Killer.
  •  Sonia reduces Cooldown by 30% for 10 seconds when overhealing by +8.
  •  Shaft reduces Cooldown by 20% during his HP-Critical ability.

Tips

[edit | edit source]
  • There is a hard cap on Cooldown reduction which is -90% and it cannot get lower than that. At -90% weapons already fire 10 times as fast. Each reduction will make it exponentially quicker, for example, if reaching -95% Cooldown with a 5% reduction were possible, it would be 20 times quicker, and another 4% reduction would be 100 times quicker. This makes each reduction from any sources very powerful and should not be seen as a simple additive bonus.
  • Cooldown reduction stacks additively. Empty Tome can provide -8% Cooldown per level, up to a total of -40%. A maxed Empty Tome combined with the Cooldown PowerUp totals -45% Cooldown. With Silent Old Sanctuary and one weapon, your Cooldown will total -85%.
  • Cooldown reduction bonuses are useful for weapons that fire slowly, and less useful for weapons that fire quickly or without Cooldown. For example,  Fire Wand benefits greatly from cooldown reduction, but  Knife may be capped out by pool limit.
